Description:

Preparation Time: 10 minutes

Cooking Time: 40 minutes

the best spaghetti bolognese you can make!

Ingredients (Serves 2):

  • 1lb / 500g Dried Spaghetti
  • 500g Ground Beef
  • 350ml Tomato Passata
  • 125ml Beef Stock
  • 125ml Red Wine
  • 75g Smoked Backon, finely diced
  • 50g Sun Dried Tomatoes, finely diced
  • 10g Freshly Grated Parmesan, plus extra to serve
  • 1 tbsp Tomato Puree
  • 2 cloves Garlic, finely diced
  • 1 large White Onion,finely diced
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire Sauce
  • Salt & Pepper, to taste
  • Olive Oil

Steps

  1. Begin by heating a large pot of salted water over high heat for the spaghetti. Once it comes to a boil, add the spaghetti and cook for 8-10 minutes, or until al dente. Drain and set aside.
  2. Meanwhile, heat some olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add the diced bacon and cook until crispy and browned. Remove the bacon and set aside.
  3. In the same skillet, add the ground beef and cook until browned and no longer pink, breaking up any large clumps with a wooden spoon or spatula.
  4. Once the beef is browned, add the onion and garlic to the skillet and saut√© until softened, about 5 minutes.
  5. Add the tomato puree, sun-dried tomatoes, Worcestershire sauce, beef stock, and red wine to the skillet. Stir well and bring to a simmer.
  6. Reduce the heat to low and simmer for 20-25 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the sauce has thickened and the flavors have melded together. Season with salt and pepper to taste.
  7. Serve the spaghetti with the bolognese sauce on top, sprinkled with the crispy bacon and grated Parmesan cheese. Enjoy!

Notes

  • For an extra special touch, serve the spaghetti bolognese with some garlic bread and a side salad.
  • You can use any type of ground meat you like in this recipe, such as turkey, pork, or lamb.
  • If you prefer a smoother sauce, you can puree the tomato passata in a blender or food processor before adding it to the skillet.
